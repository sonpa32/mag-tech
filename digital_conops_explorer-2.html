GBIA Digital ConOps — VIP / GA Terminal

6 February 2026 · GBIA ICT LTDC

System Landscape

Four interim ICT systems support all operational processes at the GBIA VIP and GA terminals. The AODB is the data backbone; CUPPS and BRS handle passenger and baggage transactions; the SOC monitors everything.

AODB
Airport Operational Database — Flight data master, manifest hub, resource allocation
Users: AOCC Operators, Duty Manager, Airport Authority
CUPPS
Common Use Passenger Processing — Check-in and boarding workstation hosting airline DCS
Users: Ground Handling Agents, Airline Agents (check-in & boarding)
BRS
Baggage Reconciliation System — Bag tracking from acceptance to aircraft loading
Users: BRS Operators, Ground Handlers (ramp, via handheld scanners)
SOC Platform
Security Operations Centre — Cybersecurity monitoring, SIEM, incident detection
Users: SOC Analysts, ICT Support

Key Observations

AODB is the backbone
Every information flow originates from or passes through the AODB. If it fails, the entire digital ecosystem degrades. RTO: 30 minutes.
VIP A creates a digital gap
VIP A passengers are exempt from CUPPS and largely from BRS. The digital ConOps for VIP A is primarily AODB record-keeping.
BRS reconciliation is the critical gate
Positive passenger–baggage matching (ICAO Annex 17) blocks departure if mismatched. The most safety-critical digital interface.
Offline resilience designed in
Handheld scanners cache 500 scans; CUPPS polls at 30s; BRS polls at 60s. Low-volume FBO tolerates intermittent connectivity.
